# DGE-METRIC Advanced Training - Track A: Content And Policy Applications

## 1. Track Purpose

This track is designed for participants who want to use DGE-METRIC results for policy analysis, scenario comparison, consultation preparation, and communication with decision makers. It keeps technical model handling present, but the main emphasis is on interpreting results, designing policy-relevant scenarios, and translating model outputs into clear messages.

Recommended use: select this track if survey responses show stronger demand for scenarios, application of results, policy interpretation, or preparation for a possible NIEF consultation.

## 3. Target Audience

- Policy analysts and advisors using DGE-METRIC evidence.
- Ministry, research, and GIZ colleagues who need to understand and explain scenario results.
- Participants who need confidence reading model outputs, comparing scenarios, and identifying policy trade-offs.
- Participants with basic exposure to DGE-METRIC and Dynare, but not necessarily a model-development role.

## 4. Learning Outcomes

By the end of Track A, participants should be able to:

1. Explain what a DGE-METRIC scenario represents and what it does not represent.
2. Compare baseline and policy scenarios using a consistent interpretation framework.
3. Identify the main transmission channels behind GDP, investment, emissions, energy price, trade balance, and financing results.
4. Draft a policy-relevant scenario note with assumptions, key indicators, interpretation, and caveats.
5. Use AI assistants to support result interpretation, narrative drafting, and documentation while verifying all claims against model outputs.
6. Prepare a concise set of results, questions, or evidence points for a consultation setting.

## 6. Session Plan

### Session 1: Policy Questions, Scenario Logic, And Indicators

Purpose:

- Align the training with concrete Vietnamese energy-transition policy questions.
- Establish a shared method for reading DGE-METRIC scenarios.
- Distinguish assumptions, mechanisms, outputs, and policy conclusions.

Core blocks:

- Welcome, online norms, and participant policy priorities.
- Recap: what DGE-METRIC adds compared with engineering or accounting models.
- Scenario anatomy: baseline, policy alternative, shock path, assumptions, model mechanism, indicators.
- Result-reading framework: result, mechanism, policy implication, caveat.
- Indicator selection: GDP, consumption, investment, emissions, energy demand, energy prices, government balance, trade balance, financing costs.
- AI-supported support task: generate an initial interpretation checklist and verify it against known model output categories.

Hands-on exercise:

- Groups choose one policy question and map the relevant scenario assumptions, expected channels, and key indicators.

Deliverable:

- Policy question and indicator map.

### Session 2: Scenario Interpretation And Trade-Offs

Purpose:

- Practice interpreting model results across policy scenarios.
- Build intuition for macro-energy transmission channels.

Core blocks:

- Recap of group policy questions.
- Scenario comparison logic: baseline vs one alternative; level vs deviation; short-run vs long-run interpretation.
- Example scenario families, depending on survey interest:
  - PDP8 / revised PDP8 baseline.
  - Net-zero pathway.
  - Rooftop solar expansion.
  - Energy efficiency pathway.
  - Financing and investment-cost assumptions.
- Common interpretation pitfalls: overclaiming causality, ignoring timing, confusing percentage changes and percentage-point changes, reading one indicator in isolation.
- AI-supported result explanation: ask an AI assistant for a draft mechanism, then verify and correct it using the actual charts/tables.

Hands-on exercise:

- Each group interprets one scenario package using a structured worksheet:
  - What changes?
  - Through which channel?
  - Who benefits or faces costs?
  - Which result is robust or uncertain?
  - What would a policymaker need to know?

Deliverable:

- Scenario interpretation sheet.

### Session 3: Policy Communication And Consultation Preparation

Purpose:

- Convert model outputs into short, decision-useful messages.
- Prepare materials that could support a consultation or technical discussion.

Core blocks:

- Selecting the right chart or table for a policy message.
- Writing a four-part result narrative: result, mechanism, implication, caveat.
- Explaining uncertainty without weakening the message.
- Turning technical caveats into transparent limitations.
- Consultation preparation: likely questions from NIEF or other stakeholders, evidence needed, open assumptions to clarify.
- AI-supported drafting: use AI to improve wording, summarize result logic, and generate possible stakeholder questions, followed by manual verification.

Hands-on exercise:

- Each group creates one policy-ready chart/table description and a short narrative for one scenario result.

Deliverable:

- One chart/table selection plus a concise policy narrative.

### Session 4: Guided Application Lab And NIEF-Readiness

Purpose:

- Apply the Track A workflow to participant-selected or organizer-prioritized topics.
- Consolidate results into outputs that can feed follow-up discussions.

Core blocks:

- Prioritized open lab topics based on survey results.
- Structured group work on selected use cases:
  - Scenario evidence for a consultation question.
  - Comparison of two energy-transition pathways.
  - Summary of macroeconomic trade-offs.
  - Financing or investment interpretation.
  - Energy efficiency or rooftop solar policy narrative.
- Peer review: clarity, evidence, mechanism, caveat, and action relevance.
- Wrap-up: unresolved assumptions, data needs, and follow-up questions.

Hands-on exercise:

- Groups prepare and present a short evidence note.

Deliverable:

- Consultation-ready evidence note with one policy question, one key result, one mechanism, one caveat, and one follow-up question.

Participants should receive:

- A short recap of the introductory DGE-METRIC training.
- Selected scenario result figures or tables.
- A simple guide to interpreting baseline vs scenario deviations.
- Optional: access to the repository for participants who want to inspect files, but this track can still work for participants who focus mainly on outputs.

## 9. Role Of AI In This Track

AI assistants should be used mainly for:

- Drafting interpretation checklists.
- Explaining possible transmission channels.
- Turning technical results into concise policy language.
- Generating likely stakeholder questions.
- Improving structure and clarity of notes.

Verification rule:

- Every AI-generated interpretation must be checked against actual model output, scenario assumptions, and trainer guidance before being used.

## 10. Completion Outputs

At the end of the track, each group should have:

- A policy question and indicator map.
- A scenario interpretation sheet.
- One policy narrative linked to a concrete result.
- One consultation-ready evidence note.
