# Activity: Chart and Narrative

Every major hands-on session produces one chart and one short policy narrative, per `../../CLAUDE.md` "Chart and policy narrative rules". Primary instance: Online Day 2 AM finance- and efficiency-scenario charts, see [`../../content/training-day-04/morning-calibration-scenario-hands-on/exercise.md`](../../content/training-day-04/morning-calibration-scenario-hands-on/exercise.md) Parts F–G. **Renamed 2026-07-13** — see [`../../design/decision-log.md`](../../design/decision-log.md).

Store chart file references and the 4-sentence narrative in [`../../scenarios/finance/scenario-log.md`](../../scenarios/finance/scenario-log.md) and [`../../scenarios/energy-efficiency/efficiency-scenario-log.md`](../../scenarios/energy-efficiency/efficiency-scenario-log.md) ("Chart and narrative references" tables). Place exported chart images/files (see [`../../assets/figures/`](../../assets/figures/)) and link them from those logs.

Chart requirements (from `../../CLAUDE.md`): clear title, baseline and scenario labels, units, time period, data source/output file, short note on assumptions. Narrative requirements: main quantitative result, economic mechanism, policy implication, one caveat, next analytical step.
