# DGE-METRIC Online Training
## Scenario Analysis, Calibration, Energy Transition & Reserve Requirements

**Continuing your DGE-METRIC training online** — this course picks up directly from the on-site training (Training Days 1–2, and Day 3 if already completed) and moves into applied, hands-on model use.

---

## Who this is for

Participants who have already attended the on-site DGE-METRIC training. This course does not repeat introductory macroeconomic or DGE modeling theory — it focuses on **using the model independently**: calibration, scenario design, interpretation, and policy communication.

## What you will be able to do afterward

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

- Navigate the DGE-METRIC repository — including tracing an equation to the code that computes it.
- Run a baseline calibration and read its diagnostics with confidence, and debug common MATLAB/Dynare errors systematically.
- Design finance scenarios with transparent, documented assumptions.
- Design and compare alternative energy transition pathways, and make a small, deliberate model modification.
- Use model outputs to inform an optimal-reserve-requirement analysis.
- Use Git for basic version control, and an AI coding assistant in VS Code to help navigate and edit model files.
- Produce reproducible charts and short, honest policy narratives — including caveats.

## Format

- **3 online sessions across 3 days: Wednesday 22 – Friday 24 July 2026** — 2 mornings and 3 afternoons of live content in total (see schedule below).
- Total live time: **approximately 15 hours** (each session runs 3 hours).
- Delivered as a mix of short conceptual walkthroughs, guided hands-on model runs, and group work — including a guided open lab on Day 3 where you apply the material to a carrier/pathway of your own choosing.
- **Software needed:** MATLAB and Dynare, installed and working before Day 1 (see "Before you join," below).
